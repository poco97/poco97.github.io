Keeping it Reel

A short documentary which explores modern perceptions of traditional film (celluloid) in an attempt to raise awareness of this dying area of filmmaking, particularly in younger generations. The crew travelled to various parts of the UK, conducting interviews with old film and cinema enthusiasts to hear their stories and to showcase their collections.

The documentary is finished and will be submitted to film festivals.

Really grateful to the interviewees: Charles Morris, Paul Wooding, David Guest, Harry Richards. We are also terribly sorry to say that David Guest has passed away couple of months after we finished the documentary. May he rest in peace.

Roles: Editor, Camera Operator

Responsibilities: On set I was gathering B-roll as well as BTS materials when needed. Being a small team and depending where we were travelling for interviews, we took turns with the B-roll camera. Nevertheless, my main role was editor. I went through at least 5 hours of raw footage to edit a 10-minute video. Some of the footage was used to create separate videos (tours of the collections). Moreover, I edited snippets to be used for promotional purposes on social media.
