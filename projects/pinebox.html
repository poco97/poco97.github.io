Pine Box by the Bedside

A social-realism short film offering an outlook on the working life of single mothers and nurses, trapped in a flawed system. Rebecca is a single mother, desperately struggling to improve her circumstances. She is caught in a tug of war between caring too much and her future career. Struggling to become a nurse, she soon finds herself with little time for her daughter. The narrative follows Rebecca throughout a normal working day, ending with her realisation of how important it is to spend time with loved ones.

The short film is still in its post-production stage, being close to getting finished. The short will also be submitted to film festivals.

Role: Producer

Responsibilities: As a producer, I was overseeing the project throughout all stages of production. I made sure the schedule was being followed and milestones met as well as make decisions to overcome unforeseen events (i.e. actors’ availability changing). Working with a small budget (around £1000), I made sure to allocate the money to each department accordingly. Moreover, I booked equipment and worked with the 1st AD to create a shooting schedule. My ‘weapon’ was the phone as I spend a long amount of time on it negotiating and securing locations. There were also a couple of film marketing events I attended to represent the film and discuss it with various people attending.
