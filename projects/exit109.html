Exit 109

A 45-minute piece which focuses on the life of Sofia, a woman who committed herself to a mental health institution, and her two fellow patients. The play delves into themes of homelessness, loss, abortion, violence and ultimately the abuse of those who are vulnerable by those who are in positions of power. Using movement, stage combat, dance, physical theatre, spoken word and music the piece is a non-linear nightmare scape inspired by the original work of Gabriella Enriocco: Ward 109.

The play is produced by NEWEN & inspired by Gabriella’s original piece: Ward 109.

Gabriella wrote Ward 109 and created Exit: 109 to raise awareness for mental health. She chose to partner with charities such as MIND Manchester and MIND Salford and 100% of the show’s revenue was donated to those charities.

Role: Camera Operator (Panasonic AG-HMC151)

Responsibilities: Having to film in a theatre, I had to adapt to the location. Two cameras were placed on the 1st level and two microphones placed strategically in order to capture the performance: a field recorder on the stage and a shotgun microphone on the 1st floor balcony. I was in charge of getting close-ups and made sure that the microphones were secure.
